Frequently Asked Questions

How much do residential solar panels cost in Nepal?

The cost of residential solar panels in Nepal typically ranges from NPR 70,000 to NPR 120,000 per kW, depending on the quality of equipment, system size, and installation complexity. Most home systems range from 3kW to 10kW, putting the total cost between NPR 2,10,000 and NPR 12,00,000.

How long do solar panels last?

Solar panels typically come with a 25-year warranty, but can last 30-35 years or more. Their efficiency gradually decreases, usually at a rate of about 0.5% per year. Most panels still produce at 80-85% of their original output even after 25 years.

What maintenance is required for solar panels?

Solar panels require minimal maintenance. Regular cleaning (2-4 times a year) to remove dust and debris is recommended to maintain optimal efficiency. Annual professional inspections can help ensure all components are functioning properly. The inverter typically needs replacement after 10-15 years.

Do solar panels work during power outages?

Standard grid-tied solar systems shut down during power outages for safety reasons. However, systems with battery storage can continue to provide electricity during outages. We offer battery backup solutions that can keep your essential appliances running even when the grid is down.
